Optima wordmark
The Optima wordmark features an allcap bespoke typeface that moves from thick to thin, heavy to light. This creates dynamic movement and a sense of direction in an otherwise clean, confident corporate type treatment. It also lets us pull out the heaviest letter, O, as an icon for various graphic treatments. The transition between weights that occurs in the wordmark creates a sense of change and growth.

Secondary wordmark
Our three main buildings are each named for colors inspired by onyx, coral and pearl – natural materials found in Mexico. Each sub-brand features the main Optima wordmark in that building’s titular color followed by its name beneath it. Each of these lockups will be used to graphically define their respective buildings, from environmental design to marketing materials to digital experiences.

Brand icon
The O becomes iconic when used across various type and logo applications. As the “O” in Tower, it connects each building’s name back to the mother brand without printing the word Optima near it. As we repeat this device across our marketing and user experience, the “O” becomes an iconic callback that keeps elements connected and concise.

Logo clear space
It is important to give the logo adequate space in the layout. The lockup should live surrounded by clear, empty space of a depth equal to the dimensions of our O icon. This allows the marque to be clearly seen and understood without interference from other graphical elements.
